Chandrashekhar Azad

Birth: 23/7/1906 | Death: 27/2/1931

Chandrashekhar Azad:


Chandrashekhar Azad(Original: Chandrashekhar Tiwari) was an Indian revelutionary who organized and led a band of youth freedom fighters during India's independence movement. While participating in Gandhi's non-cooperation movement in Benaras, he gave the name Azad (meaning free)

On 27th February 1931(today), Azad had planned to meet another revolutionary at the Alfred Park(Now named Chandrashekhar Azad Park). But, someone had betrayed him to the police.(Azad's old comrades - Veerabhadra Tiwari, and Yashpal were held responsible for tipping off the police). The police arrived at the park and surrounded it on 4 sides, and started firing. His companion, Sukhdev Raj, managed to escape uninjured, but Azad was cornered and he managed to hide behind a tree, where he began to shoot back. After a long shootout, Azad shot himself with the last bullet he had, keeping true to his pledge to always remain free, and not to be captured.
